Component-first UI speeds development

ME
Flutter Dev
• Nov 25, 2024 • 3 min read

On a recent project, I began by composing small, reusable widgets rather than constructing full screens. Within hours, page-building became almost effortless.

Each screen was simply an assembly of proven components, reducing bugs and making iterative UI work significantly faster. Small components are easier to test, read, and reuse.

The "Atomic" Mindset

A component-first approach creates long-term momentum in development. Focus on creating atomic Flutter widgets that can be styled and extended independently before moving on to complex screen layouts.

"Don't build pages. Build systems that build pages."

Key takeaway for Flutter developers:

  • Atomic Design: Break down large features into small, testable widgets.
  • Reusability: Promote widgets into a central library for quick access.
  • Performance: Smaller widget trees are generally easier for Flutter's rendering engine to optimize.

By isolating logic into smaller units (`StatelessWidget`), you also make your codebase significantly more testable. If a button works in isolation, it will work on the Login page, the Profile page, and the Settings page.
